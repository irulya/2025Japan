Long Story: 545 PM - Step aside

A short step from the crossing, Hachiko’s bronze figure waits—tiny amid the bustle. Unveiled 1935, it marks a dog’s decade-long vigil for his dead owner, a story etched in Japan’s heart. Crowds circle, cameras click; it’s a quiet counterpoint to Shibuya’s roar—loyalty cast in metal, still standing.
