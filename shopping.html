Affordable, High-Quality Clothing in Tokyo

Tsukiji Outer Market (Day 3, 12:00 PM - 1:30 PM)

Where: 500m walk from Tsukiji Station, Chuo City.

What: Primarily a food market, but some stalls sell packaged goods like high-quality cotton aprons or fish-themed tees—durable, simple stuff. Think ¥500-1,000 for items that last. Not fashion-central, but the quality’s solid for casual wear.

Why: It’s more about utility than style—practical pieces from a historic market.

Senso-ji Temple - Nakamise Street (Day 3, 1:30 PM - 2:30 PM)

Where: 500m walk from Asakusa Station, Taito City.

What: Traditional Japanese goods along a 300m stretch—think handheld fans (¥1,000-2,000), cotton yukata tops (¥2,000-5,000), or sturdy senbei snack bags. Quality’s high due to craftsmanship roots from the 1600s.

Why: Affordable souvenirs that double as wearable art—light, breathable fabrics perfect for daily use.

Akihabara Electric Town (Day 3, 2:30 PM - 4:00 PM)

Where: 2 km walk from Senso-ji, Chiyoda City.

What: Anime-inspired clothing like graphic tees (¥500-2,000) and hoodies (¥3,000-5,000) from shops like Animate. Also, techy accessories—LED caps or bags (¥1,000-3,000). Quality’s decent—thick cotton, good stitching.

Why: Budget-friendly for otaku vibes, and the durability matches the price—great for casual, quirky wear.

Takeshita Street (Day 3, 4:00 PM - 5:00 PM)

Where: 1 km walk from Yoyogi Station, Shibuya City.

What: Kawaii fashion galore—tees, skirts, and hoodies from WEGO or indie stalls (¥500-2,000). Higher-end lolita dresses at 6%DOKIDOKI (¥5,000-10,000) still feel premium for the cost. Fabrics are often cotton-poly blends, built to last a few seasons.

Why: Trendy, youthful styles at fast-fashion prices—quality’s above chain-store norms like H&M.

Affordable, High-Quality Footwear in Tokyo

Akihabara Electric Town

What: Sneakers from Yodobashi Camera—think discounted Adidas or Asics (¥5,000-10,000). Quality’s high—Japanese brands prioritize durability and comfort.

Why: Affordable access to Japan’s top athletic labels, often cheaper than overseas.

Takeshita Street

What: Funky platform shoes or boots from indie shops (¥3,000-7,000). Also, basic sneakers at WEGO (¥2,000-4,000)—solid rubber soles, decent cushioning.

Why: Unique designs with better-than-expected build—perfect for statement pieces that hold up.

Extra Tips

Secondhand Scene: Beyond Day 3, Shimokitazawa (not in the itinerary) is a thrifting hub—vintage Levi’s or Japanese brands like Beams go for ¥2,000-5,000. Quality’s top-notch due to Japan’s care for used goods.

Chains: Uniqlo (various locations) offers basics—tees (¥1,000), chinos (¥3,000)—with tech fabrics like AIRism that punch above their price. GU is even cheaper (¥500-2,000) and still reliable.
