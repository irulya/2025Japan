Long Story: 10 AM - Wake up slow

The clock hits 10, and your body’s still on yesterday’s time zone—legs heavy, eyes gritty. But the Airbnb’s cozy, and a 7-Eleven’s just steps away. The teens lead the charge, snagging onigiri—salmon for one, tuna for the other, while adults sip tea, watching steam curl up. Outside, Ōkubo’s Koreatown hums: BBQ smoke wafts from grills, K-pop posters plaster walls, and a shop blasts BTS. It’s a gentle jolt—Tokyo’s alive, and you’re in it now.
