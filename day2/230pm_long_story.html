Long Story: 230 PM - Ride south

The train zips south, Tokyo morphing from Shinjuku’s chaos to Roppongi’s sleek vibe. At Mori Tower’s 52nd floor, glass walls frame a mind-bending view: Shibuya’s neon veins, Skytree’s distant spear, maybe Fuji’s snowcap if clouds play nice. Downstairs, Mori Garden waits—cherry blossoms sway, koi glide, and a massive spider sculpture stands out, art gone wild. Coffee and pastries fuel a break. It’s Tokyo in layers: sky-high dreams, grounded quirks.
