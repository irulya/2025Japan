Day 1 (03/31/25): Alternate Routes to Airbnb

  • Airport Limousine Bus + Taxis: This combines the bus to Shinjuku Station West Exit with a short taxi ride to your Airbnb.
    • Bus Details:
      • Route: Haneda Airport (Terminal 3) to Shinjuku Station West Exit
      • Schedule: Buses run every 15-30 minutes. Post-customs (30-60 minutes after 1:05 PM), you’d board around 2:00-2:30 PM (e.g., a 2:30 PM bus)
      • Travel Time: 35-60 minutes, arriving at Shinjuku by 3:05-3:30 PM
      • Cost: 1,400 JPY per adult, and your 13- and 15-year-olds are charged adult rates. Total: 6 × 1,400 = 8,400 JPY
      • Luggage: Six suitcases should fit under the bus (two pieces per passenger allowed), though space isn’t guaranteed if crowded
      • Booking: Reserve via Airport Limousine Bus Reservation. Cancellation up to 5 minutes before departure incurs a 110 JPY fee per ticket (660 JPY total)
    • Taxi Details:
      • Route: Shinjuku Station West Exit to 2-chōme-19-9 Ōkubo (1-1.5 km, 5-10 minutes)
      • Cost: Two standard taxis (3 people + 3 suitcases each) at ~700-1,000 JPY each. Total: 1,400-2,000 JPY
    • Total Cost: 8,400 (bus) + 1,400-2,000 (taxis) = 9,800-10,400 JPY
    • Total Time: Depart Haneda at 2:30 PM, arrive Shinjuku by 3:30 PM, then 5-10 minutes by taxi, reaching your Airbnb by 3:40-4:00 PM
    • Pros: Cheapest option, comfortable bus with luggage storage, flexible cancellation
    • Cons: Two-step process, potential bus crowding, short taxi coordination needed
  • Jumbo Taxi: A single large vehicle (e.g., Toyota HiAce) takes you directly from Haneda to your Airbnb.
    • Details:
      • Route: Haneda Airport (Terminal 3) to 2-chōme-19-9 Ōkubo
      • Travel Time: 30-50 minutes, depending on traffic. Departing at 2:15 PM post-customs, you’d arrive by 2:45-3:05 PM
      • Cost: Base rate for a HiAce to Shinjuku for six passengers is 21,000 JPY. Tolls (~1,500-2,000 JPY) add up, making it 21,000-23,000 JPY total
      • Luggage: Easily fits six suitcases and six people
      • Booking: Pre-book via Tokyo MK or similar (e.g., Nihon Kotsu). Cancellation policies vary by provider but typically allow refunds with notice
    • Total Cost: 21,000-23,000 JPY (base + tolls)
    • Total Time: Arrive by 2:45-3:05 PM
    • Pros: Fastest, door-to-door, no transfers, ample space for all
    • Cons: Significantly more expensive, requires pre-booking for best rates
  • Two Standard Taxis: Splitting into two groups for the direct trip.
    • Details:
      • Route: Haneda to 2-chōme-19-9 Ōkubo
      • Travel Time: 30-50 minutes, arriving 2:45-3:05 PM
      • Cost: Each taxi is 7,500 JPY (flat rate) to 9,000-10,000 JPY (metered with tolls). Total: 15,000-18,000 JPY
      • Luggage: Three suitcases + three people per taxi
    • Total Cost: 15,000-18,000 JPY
    • Pros: Direct, no booking needed, cheaper than jumbo taxi
    • Cons: Splits the group, higher than bus option
  • Recommendation:
    • Best Value: Limousine Bus + Taxis (9,800-10,400 JPY). Most economical, saving 5,000-13,000 JPY over taxi options. Book for a 2:30 PM departure
    • Best Convenience: Jumbo Taxi (21,000-23,000 JPY). Fastest and simplest—direct by 2:45-3:05 PM—but double the bus cost. Pre-book it
    • Middle Ground: Two Standard Taxis (15,000-18,000 JPY). Direct and available, but less cohesive for your family
