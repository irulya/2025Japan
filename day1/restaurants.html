Day 1 (03/31/25): Dining Options Near Airbnb

  • Dining Options Near 2-chōme-19-9 Ōkubo: Your Airbnb is in Ōkubo, a vibrant area in Shinjuku known for its Korean influence but also packed with Japanese dining options. It’s near Shin-Ōkubo Station (one stop from Shinjuku on the Yamanote Line) and within walking distance of diverse eateries. Given your group’s preferences—sushi, ramen, eel, and pescatarian needs—here are some tailored suggestions:
    • Sushi: Kura Sushi Shinjuku Kabukicho:
      • Location: Kabukicho, Shinjuku (about 1 km from your Airbnb, 15-minute walk or 5-minute taxi) - Google Maps Route
      • Why It Fits: This is a popular conveyor belt sushi chain, affordable and kid-friendly. It offers a wide variety of sushi, including fish options like tuna, salmon, and shrimp that suit pescatarians, plus fun extras like tamago (egg) and cucumber rolls for kids. Plates start at ¥100-¥200, and there’s a touchscreen ordering system
      • Family-Friendly: Spacious seating, quick service, and a gimmick where kids can play a game after eating a set number of plates (win a small toy). Open late (usually 11 AM to 11 PM), perfect for your arrival timing
      • Pescatarian Note: Plenty of seafood and veggie options; no need to worry about meat-heavy dishes
    • Ramen: Ichiran Shinjuku Kabukicho:
      • Location: Kabukicho, Shinjuku (1.2 km from your Airbnb, 15-20 minute walk or short taxi) - Google Maps Route
      • Why It Fits: Ichiran is a famous ramen chain specializing in tonkotsu (pork broth) ramen, but you can customize it. Pescatarians can request no pork slices and stick to egg, seaweed, and noodle-focused bowls, or ask about broth alternatives (some locations offer lighter options). Kids love the slurpy noodles and booth-style seating
      • Family-Friendly: Private booths let your group eat comfortably together. Open 24 hours, so it’s flexible for jet-lagged schedules
      • Pescatarian Tip: Double-check with staff for a fish-based broth option (less common but sometimes available) or focus on toppings like nori and green onions
    • Eel (Unagi): Unagi Sansho:
      • Location: Near Shinjuku Station (1.5 km from your Airbnb, 20-minute walk or quick taxi) - Google Maps Route
      • Why It Fits: This spot serves delicious unagi (grilled freshwater eel) over rice, a treat your whole group can enjoy. Pescatarians will love it, and it’s a light yet flavorful option after travel. They also offer side dishes like miso soup and pickled veggies
      • Family-Friendly: Traditional seating with tables (some tatami, call ahead to reserve if needed). Kids might enjoy the sweet-savory eel sauce. Typically open for lunch and dinner (check hours, often 11:30 AM-2 PM, 5-9 PM)
      • Note: Unagi is pricier (¥2,000-¥3,000 per bowl), but it’s a special treat
    • Flexible Pescatarian Option: Tonkatsu Maisen (Shinjuku Takashimaya Branch):
      • Location: Shinjuku Takashimaya, 14th floor (1.8 km from your Airbnb, 25-minute walk or 10-minute taxi) - Google Maps Route
      • Why It Fits: Known for tonkatsu (breaded pork cutlets), Maisen also offers ebi-fry (fried shrimp) and katsu-curry with seafood options, perfect for pescatarians. Non-pescatarians can enjoy pork, and kids can have smaller portions or rice with sides
      • Family-Friendly: Located in a department store with a casual dining vibe, it has plenty of seating and a varied menu. Open 11 AM-10 PM
      • Bonus: Great views of Shinjuku from the 14th floor
    • Casual All-Around: Haneda Ichiba Shinjuku:
      • Location: Near Shinjuku Station (1.5 km from your Airbnb) - Google Maps Route
      • Why It Fits: This place specializes in fresh seafood—sushi, sashimi, and grilled fish—straight from Haneda’s market. Pescatarians will thrive here, and there’s eel on the menu too. Non-pescatarians can try fish-based ramen or rice bowls
      • Family-Friendly: Bright, welcoming atmosphere with table seating. Open for lunch and dinner (typically 11 AM-11 PM)
      • Tip: Try the kaisendon (seafood rice bowl) for variety
    • Practical Tips:
      • Timing: Arriving around 3 PM, you might want a late lunch. Most places listed are open all day or reopen by 5 PM for dinner
      • Walking: Ōkubo to Kabukicho or Shinjuku Station is walkable (10-25 minutes), but with kids and luggage, a taxi (¥500-¥1,000) might be easier post-flight
      • Reservations: Not always needed, but for a group of 6, calling ahead (or asking your Airbnb host to help) ensures seating, especially at Unagi Sansho or Maisen
      • Budget: Expect ¥1,000-¥2,000 per adult at casual spots like Kura or Ichiran, more (¥2,500-¥4,000) at unagi or seafood restaurants
      • Kid-Friendly Extras: Many spots offer small portions or rice-based dishes for kids. Bring a few snacks from Haneda in case they’re picky post-flight
